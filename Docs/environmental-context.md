# ENVIRONMENTAL CONTEXT

## Mozambique's Environmental Landscape

### Geographic Overview:
- **Area**: 801,590 km²
- **Coastline**: 2,470 km along Indian Ocean
- **Major ecosystems**: Coastal mangroves, miombo woodlands, savannas, floodplains
- **Biodiversity**: Home to 5,600+ plant species and 1,000+ vertebrate species

### Environmental Challenges:
1. **Deforestation**: Annual loss of 0.5% forest cover
2. **Climate change**: Increased frequency of droughts and floods
3. **Biodiversity loss**: Habitat fragmentation and species endangerment
4. **Water resource management**: Seasonal variability and pollution
5. **Soil degradation**: Erosion and nutrient depletion

## Why Environmental Monitoring Matters

### Conservation Priorities:
- **Protected areas**: Covering 26% of national territory
- **Endangered species**: Including African elephants, black rhinos, dugongs
- **Critical habitats**: Coastal ecosystems, wetlands, forests
- **Climate resilience**: Adaptation to changing environmental conditions

### Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) Alignment:
- **SDG 13**: Climate Action - Monitoring environmental changes
- **SDG 14**: Life Below Water - Coastal ecosystem monitoring
- **SDG 15**: Life on Land - Terrestrial ecosystem protection
- **SDG 6**: Clean Water - Water resource monitoring

## Conclusion

This environmental monitoring station represents a practical, affordable solution for addressing Mozambique's environmental monitoring needs. By combining simple technology with scientific methodology, it enables data-driven conservation and sustainable development while building local capacity in environmental science and technology.

The system's modular design allows for adaptation to various environmental contexts and expansion with additional sensors, making it a versatile tool for ecological research, conservation planning, and environmental education across Mozambique and beyond.
